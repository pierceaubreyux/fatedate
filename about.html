Our story

Built out of frustration with the status quo

The idea for FateDate started with a simple observation: every dating app is optimised to keep you on the app, not to help you find someone. The longer you stay single, the more valuable you are to them.

We wanted to flip that. Our success is measured by the number of people who delete the app because they found someone — not by monthly active users or swipe counts.

So we built something end-to-end. You verify your identity, you connect through games designed to reveal personality, and you book a date at a real venue. The whole thing is designed to get you off your phone and in front of someone.

“Finally an app that actually wants you to meet.”

What we stand for

The principles behind every decision we make

Safety first

Every member is ID-verified before they can message anyone. We made this non-negotiable from day one, even when it added friction to onboarding.

Your time matters

The app is built to move you toward a real date as quickly as possible. Every feature is judged against one question: does this help people actually meet?

Real people, real places

We only work with venues we’d actually want to go on a date at ourselves. Every partner is vetted by the team — no pay-to-play, no exceptions.

Honesty over hype

We don’t show inflated match counts or fake activity. If there aren’t many people near you right now, we’ll tell you. We’d rather earn your trust than lose it.

Privacy by design

Your data is used to make your experience better. That’s it. We don’t sell it, we don’t share it with advertisers, and we never will.

Built to last

We’re not chasing growth at any cost. We’re building something that still works in ten years — for the people who use it and for the venues who trust us.
