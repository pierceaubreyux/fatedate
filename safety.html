Safety

Safer dating tips

Getting to know someone online is part of the process — but that doesn't mean sharing everything. Here's what we recommend to keep you safe before, during, and after every date.

Protect your personal information

01
Keep private things private Keep your workplace, home address, details about any children, and daily routines to yourself until you genuinely trust the person you're talking to. There's no rush.

Stay on the FateDate platform

02
Be cautious about moving off-app early Be wary of anyone who asks for your number or email address right away, before you've even met. Moving the conversation away from FateDate means we can't support your safety in the same way when it comes to meeting up.
03
Use the DateDiary™ to arrange dates We always recommend using the DateDiary™ feature to confirm and book dates. It's a safer way to arrange things, and you'll also get access to discounted dates at our handpicked partner venues.

Meeting up safely

04
Always meet in public — especially the first time All dates, particularly first dates, should take place in a public venue. Arrange to meet your date there rather than being picked up — never give a stranger your home address.

Tell someone you trust

05
Let a friend or family member know where you're going Before any date — especially a first one — tell someone you trust where you're going, when, and who you're meeting. Keep their number saved on a fully charged phone before you leave.

Watch your drink

06
Never leave your drink unattended Alcohol can lower your guard — be mindful of how much you drink. Never leave your drink unattended, take it with you if you need to step away, and keep it in view even at the bar. Hold your glass where you can see it clearly.
07
If you suspect drink spiking, act immediately If you believe you or someone nearby has been a victim of drink spiking, or you suspect someone is attempting it, tell a member of staff at the venue straight away.

Ending a date early

08
Use the app to end your date discreetly If you need to leave early, step away from your date, open the DateDiary™ feature, and end the date. The other person will receive a notification that the date has ended — no awkward conversation needed.
09
Leave a venue review If you feel comfortable ending the date face to face, that's great. Either way, please leave a review of the venue once you're home — it helps the whole community.

If you feel unsafe

10
Let your safe contact know straight away If something doesn't feel right, message your trusted friend or family member immediately.
Ask for Angela

If you feel uncomfortable or threatened at a venue, ask a member of bar or till staff for "Angela". This is a nationwide scheme that signals to trained staff that you need help — discreetly and without drawing attention.

In immediate danger — call 999 If you are in serious danger, call 999 immediately and seek help. Your safety is always the priority.

Tips for a great date

Safety sorted — here's how to give yourself the best chance of a good time.

Dress to impress First impressions matter Smile Arrive in a good mood A small gift goes a long way

Whatever the occasion, dress appropriately and put thought into your appearance — it shows you care. A smile and a relaxed attitude will always make the evening easier. Personal gestures like flowers are a lovely touch, but never a requirement.
