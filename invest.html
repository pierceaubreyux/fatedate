The opportunity

A broken market, ready to be rebuilt

£8.4bn UK dating market by 2028

The online dating industry continues to grow year on year — but satisfaction with existing platforms is falling. The gap between what people want and what apps deliver has never been wider.

73% Of users feel burned out by apps

Research consistently shows that dating app fatigue is at an all-time high. People are still looking for relationships — they've just stopped believing apps can help them find one.

0 End-to-end verified dating platforms

No platform currently combines full ID verification, structured connection, and real-world venue booking into a single experience. That's the gap FateDate was built to fill.

The problem with every other dating app

Tinder, Hinge, Bumble — they're all optimised for engagement, not outcomes. Their revenue depends on you staying single. The longer you swipe, the more you spend. That misalignment between user goals and business model is the root of everything people hate about modern dating.

Add in the safety problem — fake profiles, catfishing, no accountability — and you have an industry that has built enormous scale on a deeply broken foundation.

What we're building instead

FateDate charges a subscription to access the platform. Our revenue goes up when people find the app valuable enough to keep paying — not when they fail to find a relationship. We succeed when our users succeed.

Every member is ID-verified. Every date happens at a real venue we've vetted. Every interaction is designed to move toward an actual meeting, not another week of messaging. It's a fundamentally different model — and we believe it's the right one for what comes next.
